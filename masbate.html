About Masbate

Masbate, part of the Bicol Region, is known as the "Cattle Capital of the Philippines." Once home to thriving indigenous communities, it became a center for cattle ranching during Spanish colonization. Masbate’s unique charm and high-quality products make it a prime destination for travelers and a source of the best pasalubong in Bicol.

Best Pasalubong from Masbate

Carmelado

‘Carmelado’ is a milk candy with a sweet and rich flavor made from carabao milk and sugar. The candy is cheap and easily available in Masbate and has become a favorite ‘pasalubong,’ or souvenir treat. Its name originates from ‘carmelada,’ a Spanish word meaning caramelized.

There is no need to crave , for your sweet indulgence – carmelado can be bought at the airport and the seaport.

Molido

One of the famous pasalubong that you shouldn't miss whenever you visit Masbate is this sweet delicacy called Molido. Molido, a Spanish word that means "ground" or "milled", is a dessert made from mashed boiled sweet potato (Kamote) which is common in the markets of Masbate especially during flea market day (saud). Aside from that, it can also be made using Pili/nuts, squash, or sometimes sprinkled with coconut bits for additional flavor. This sweet treat is available in varying consistency, it can soft, chewy, or hard.

Feel the sweetness of Molido inside your mouth that will surely arouse your taste buds.

Masbate Leathercraft

Masbate Province is home to artisans who produce high-quality leather products ranging from small tags and keychains to bags, belts, and shoes. This is a perfect Pasalubong for leathercraft lovers.
