About Sorsogon

Sorsogon, the southernmost gem of Luzon, boasts a history as vibrant as its landscapes. Once a thriving hub for early Austronesian settlers and Asian traders, it became a significant province during Spanish colonization in the 16th century. The Spaniards introduced Christianity, leaving a legacy seen in its historic churches. Over the centuries, Sorsogon grew into a land of cultural richness and natural wonders, from serene beaches to lush mountains. Today, it is famed not only for its breathtaking scenery but also for its delightful pasalubong, including pili nut treats and handcrafted mats, embodying the heart and soul of this enchanting province.

Chili Cupcakes

Chili Cupcakes are a delightful and daring twist on traditional chocolate cupcakes, making them a must-try treat when in Sorsogon. These cupcakes perfectly blend the rich, velvety flavor of chocolate with the unique spiciness of Bicolano sili (chili) and the subtle nuttiness of pili nuts, a prized delicacy of the region. The result is a dessert that’s both sweet and spicy, capturing the bold and vibrant flavors that Bicol is famous for.This is a best Pasalubong for those spicy lovers.

Available at Gahfea Café and Bistro G/F, Gaisano Capital, Magsaysay St., Sorsogon City

NONO Bakery Yema Buko Pie

When visiting Sorsogon, don’t miss the iconic Yema Buko Pie from Nono Bakery—a unique twist on the classic favorite. Unlike traditional buko pies that are best served warm, this delicacy is best enjoyed chilled, offering a refreshing and creamy blend of sweet yema and tender coconut filling. Its perfect balance of flavors and textures makes it a standout treat and the perfect pasalubong to share with your loved ones.

You can find Nono Bakery conveniently located at Market Site, Brgy. Sirangan, Sorsogon City, right in front of Jeanne's Supermarket. For orders and reservations, contact: 0919-662-8692/0927-032-1957. Take home a taste of Sorsogon with this unforgettable specialty!
